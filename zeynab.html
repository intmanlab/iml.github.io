    Collaborating with IML since 2019


    Dr Zeynab Talebpour
    Remote collaborator on Deep learning and computer vision -- Lincoln Agri-Robotic,
    Post-doctoral Fellow at University of British Columbia (UBC), Vancouver/ Robotics and AI scientist at AIS Inc.
    updated: 20.05.2021
    Research interests: Multi-robot systems, Deep learning, Computer Vision, Robot perception, Human-Robot Interaction, Multi-robot Coordination and planning.


    Short Biography

    I am currently a postdoctoral Fellow at UBC in a program between academia and industry, and a remote collaborator at IML. I work on vision-based solutions with a focus on deep learning for improved robot situational-awareness. I received my Ph.D. degree in robotics, control and intelligent systems from École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL), Switzerland in 2018. During my Ph.D. and Post-doctoral research at DISAL, I developed an end-to-end framework for cooperative human-aware navigation and coordination of multi-robot systems in social environments. I received my M.Sc. in machine learning and robotics in 2012 and my B.Sc. in computer software engineering in 2009 from University of Tehran.
