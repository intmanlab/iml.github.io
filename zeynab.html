    Dr Zeynab Talebpour
    Remote collaborator on Deep learning and computer vision -- Lincoln Agri-Robotic,
    Machine Learning and AI research scientist at PPRC in Djavad Mowafaghian Centre for Brain Health at UBC
    updated: 10.08.2021
    Research interests: Deep learning, Machine learning, Multi-robot systems, Computer Vision, Neuroimaging, Robot perception, Human-Robot Interaction, Multi-robot Coordination and planning.


    Short Biography

    I am currently a Machine Learning and AI research scientist at PPRC in Djavad Mowafaghian Centre for Brain Health at UBC, and a remote collaborator at IML. Prior to that, I've worked on human-aware multi-robot systmes and vision-based solutions with a focus on deep learning for improved robot situational-awareness. I received my Ph.D. degree in Robotics, Control and Intelligent systems from École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL), Switzerland in 2018. During my Ph.D. and Post-doctoral research at DISAL, I developed an end-to-end framework for cooperative human-aware navigation and coordination of multi-robot systems in social environments. I received my M.Sc. in machine learning and robotics in 2012 and my B.Sc. in computer software engineering in 2009 from University of Tehran.
